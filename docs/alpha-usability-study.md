# Headroom household alpha study

The alpha question is simple: can a person who does not think in file-system terms understand what is using room, choose a safe action, and correctly describe what happened afterward?

## Participants

Recruit 5–8 Mac owners who are not developers. Include at least two people who have previously hit a “disk full” warning and at least two who use iCloud Drive. Use their own Macs and real storage state; do not seed fake recommendations.

## Session

Allow 30 minutes. Ask the participant to think aloud, but do not teach the interface until a task is clearly blocked.

1. “Tell me whether your Mac has enough room right now.”
2. “Show me what is using the most room in the folders Headroom can see.”
3. “Explain one suggestion in your own words. What will happen, and can you undo it?”
4. “Choose a cleanup you feel comfortable doing. Review every included file.”
5. For a Trash action, empty Trash only if the participant is comfortable, then use **Check results**.
6. “Tell me the difference between space Headroom says could be freed and space it observed afterward.”
7. “Protect something you never want Headroom to suggest again.”
8. If anything is confusing, use the suggestion menu to record why and export diagnostics from Settings.

## Observe without leading

- First words used to describe storage health
- Whether “safe reserve” and “could be freed” are understood
- Hesitation before review and approval
- Any belief that moving to Trash immediately frees disk space
- Any category or application label the participant cannot interpret
- Suggestions dismissed as unsafe, unclear, irrelevant, or still needed
- Whether the participant can recover from an interrupted scan or unavailable folder

## Success bar

- 80% correctly identify current free space and storage health without help.
- 80% can explain a recommendation’s evidence, consequence, and undo path.
- 0 participants believe Headroom permanently deletes files automatically.
- 80% complete one safe cleanup and find its pending or verified outcome.
- Median time from opening the app to choosing a useful suggestion is under five minutes.
- Every trust incident is treated as release-blocking until understood.

## Session record

Record participant ID, Mac model/macOS, storage state, task outcomes, time to first useful suggestion, accepted/dismissed recommendation kinds, comprehension failures, trust incidents, and the path to an exported diagnostics file. Do not copy file names or paths into research notes without explicit permission.
